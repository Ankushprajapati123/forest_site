Monkey

Common Names: various mammals within the infraorder Simiiformes Scientific Name: Macaca mulatta Monkeys are intelligent, social mammals known for their agility and adaptability, belonging to the primate order. They are characterized by their long limbs and tails, which aid in climbing and swinging through trees. There are roughly 200 different species, classified as either Old World or New World monkeys, with distinct characteristics and geographical distributions.

Elephant

Common Names:African bush elephant, African forest elephant, and Asian elephant Scientific Name:Elephantidae Elephants are the largest living land animals and are categorized into three species: the African bush elephant, the African forest elephant, and the Asian elephant. They are the only surviving members of the family Elephantidae and the order Proboscidea, whose extinct relatives include mammoths and mastodons.

Peacock

Common Names: Peacock Scientific Name:Pavo cristatus The Indian peacock (Pavo cristatus) is the national bird of India, known for its vibrant blue and green plumage and the impressive train of feathers on the male. Peacocks are omnivores, eating insects, plants, and small animals, and they are ground-dwelling birds that are not built for sustained flight.

Parrot

Common Names:Parrot Scientific Name: Pasittaciformes Parrots are a diverse group of birds characterized by their vibrant plumage, strong, curved beaks, and intelligence. They are known for their ability to mimic sounds, including human speech. Parrots are found in warm climates around the world and vary greatly in size, from the tiny pygmy parrot to the large macaws.

sparrow

Common Names:sparrow Scientific Name:Passer domesticus This sparrow is characterized by its rich chestnut crown and nape, and a distinctive black patch on each pure white cheek. The sexes are similarly plumaged, though young birds have duller colors.

crocodile

Common Names:crocodile Scientific Name:Crocodylidae A crocodile is a large, predatory, semi-aquatic reptile known for its powerful jaws, tough skin, and ability to live in tropical regions. They are found in Africa, Asia, the Americas, and Australia. Crocodiles are part of the order Crocodilia, which also includes alligators, caimans, and gharials.

butterfly

Common Names:butterfly Scientific Name:Lepidoptera A butterfly is a winged insect known for its large, often colorful wings and distinctive, fluttering flight. They belong to the order Lepidoptera, which also includes moths. Butterflies are celebrated for their beauty and metamorphosis, transitioning from a caterpillar to a pupa (chrysalis), and finally to an adult butterfly.
