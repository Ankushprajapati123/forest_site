Problem of forests

  1. Deforestation
    2. Deforestation or forest clearance is the removal and destruction of a forest or stand of trees from land that is then converted to non-forest use.[1] Deforestation can involve conversion of forest land to farms, ranches, or urban use. About 31% of Earth's land surface is covered by forests at present.[2] This is one-third less than the forest cover before the expansion of agriculture, with half of that loss occurring in the last century.[3] Between 15 million to 18 million hectares of forest, an area the size of Bangladesh, are destroyed every year. On average 2,400 trees are cut down each minute.

  2. Illegal logging
    3. To cut wood, you must first make sure you have the necessary permission, especially if you are cutting wood from public land or a forested area. Next, you need to cut the wood using appropriate tools and safety measures .

  3. forest fires
    4. Forest fires, also known as wildfires, are uncontrolled and unmanaged blazes that consume vegetation in natural settings like forests, grasslands, and tundra. These fires are fueled by a combination of natural and human-related causes, including lightning strikes, high temperatures, and human carelessness. The spread of a forest fire depends on factors like wind, topography, and the availability of dry fuel like leaves and dead wood.

  4. Urbanization
    5. Urbanization is a process in which a large portion of the population moves from rural areas to urban areas, causing cities to grow both in size and population. It is an ever-changing process that creates a contrast between rural and urban life.
