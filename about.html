About Panna Tiger Reserve

A forest is a large area dominated by trees and other woody vegetation. It's a vital ecosystem that provides numerous benefits, including maintaining the planet's balance, providing habitat for various species, and regulating climate. Forests are crucial for human well-being, offering resources like timber, food, and medicines, as well as essential environmental services like carbon sequestration and soil protection.

Forests are broadly classified into three major types: tropical, temperate, and boreal. These categories are primarily determined by latitude and climate, influencing the types of trees and ecosystems found in each region. Within these broad categories, there are various sub-types of forests, such as evergreen, deciduous, coniferous, and mangrove forests, each with unique characteristics.

  • Tropical Rainforests: Characterized by high rainfall, high biodiversity, and warm temperatures year-round.
  • Tropical Moist Deciduous Forests: Found in areas with distinct wet and dry seasons, with trees shedding leaves during the dry period.
  • Tropical Dry Deciduous Forests: Have a longer dry season and lower rainfall than moist deciduous forests.
  • Tropical Thorn Forests: Adapted to arid conditions with thorny trees and shrubs.
  • Mangrove Forests: Found in coastal intertidal zones, with specialized trees that can tolerate saltwater.
  • Temperate Deciduous Forests: Experience four distinct seasons, with trees shedding leaves in the fall.
  • Temperate Coniferous Forests: Dominated by cone-bearing evergreen trees like pines and firs.
  • Mediterranean Forests: Found in regions with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers.
  • Montane Forests: Located in mountainous areas, with varying vegetation types depending on altitude and climate.
