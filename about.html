About Panna Tiger Reserve

Importance of Forests

Forests are essential ecosystems that play a crucial role in maintaining Earth's balance. They provide shelter for diverse wildlife, regulate climate, purify air and water, and support human life with resources like timber, food, and medicine.

Classification of Forests

Forests are generally divided into tropical, temperate, and boreal types. Their classification depends on climate and geography, which in turn shape the types of flora and fauna found in each.

Tropical Forests

  • Tropical Rainforests: Hot and humid with dense biodiversity.
  • Tropical Moist Deciduous Forests: Trees shed leaves in dry season.
  • Tropical Dry Deciduous Forests: Less rainfall and longer dry seasons.
  • Tropical Thorn Forests: Thorny plants adapted to arid conditions.
  • Mangrove Forests: Found in salty coastal zones, rich in marine life.

Temperate Forests

  • Temperate Deciduous Forests: Four seasons, colorful foliage in autumn.
  • Temperate Coniferous Forests: Evergreen trees like pines and firs.
  • Mediterranean Forests: Hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters.
  • Montane Forests: Found in mountain ranges, changing with altitude.

Boreal Forests

These forests lie in high northern latitudes, dominated by cold-tolerant coniferous trees. They withstand long winters and brief summers and are vital carbon sinks.

Other Forest Types

  • Mangrove Forests: Specialized trees in tidal, saline environments.
  • Montane Forests: Changing tree species with elevation.
  • Mediterranean Forests: Resistant to drought and fire-prone conditions.
  • Cloud Forests: High-altitude forests shrouded in mist, rich in mosses and orchids.
