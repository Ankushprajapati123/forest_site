  <h1> Bamboo </h1>

  1. Introduction: Bamboo is a fast-growing, woody grass belonging to the Poaceae family. Although it resembles a tree,
  it is technically a grass and is known for its strength, flexibility, and eco-friendly qualities. There are over 1,400
  species of bamboo found across the world, particularly in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.
  <p>


  <div id="three">

    Scientific Classification:<br>

    Kingdom: Plantae<br>

    Order: Poales<br>

    Family: Poaceae<br>

    Subfamily: Bambusoideae<br>

    Common Name: amboo<br>

  <p>Features of Bamboo:<br>

    Growth Rate: One of the fastest-growing plants in the world; some species can grow up to 91 cm (35 in) per
    day.<br><br>
B
    Structure: Hollow stem (called culm) with nodes and internodes.<br><br>

    Height: Can range from a few feet to over 30 meters (98 feet) depending on species.<br><br>

    Leaves: Long, narrow, and green with parallel venation.<br><br>

    Roots: Rhizome-based underground system, which helps in quick spreading.</p>
