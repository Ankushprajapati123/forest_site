Water hyacinth

Common Names: Jalkumbhi (Hindi), Water hyacinth(English)
Scientific Name: Eichhornia crassipes
Water hyacinth (Eichhornia crassipes) is a free-floating aquatic plant known for its rapid growth and potential to become a problematic invasive species. It's characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and showy lavender-blue flowers with a yellow spot. While it can be beneficial in some ways, like removing pollutants from water, it also poses significant ecological and economic challenges

Bamboo

Neem Tree (Azadirachta indica)

Common Names: Neem, Indian Lilac
Scientific Name: Azadirachta indica
Neem, also known as Indian Lilac or Margosa, is a highly valued evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia. It is renowned for its diverse uses and medicinal properties, considered a "wonder tree" or "natural pesticide".

Rose

Common Names: Rose
Scientific Name: Rosa
Roses are flowering shrubs or vines in the genus Rosa, known for their beauty and fragrance. They belong to the Rosaceae family and are native to temperate and subtropical regions of the Northern Hemisphere. There are over 300 species and tens of thousands of cultivars, with variations in size, shape, and color.

Tulsi

Common Names:Tulsi
Scientific Name: Ocimum sanctum
Tulsi, also known as Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum or Ocimum sanctum), is a revered aromatic plant in Hinduism and has significant medicinal properties in Ayurvedic practices. It's a small, branched, and hairy herb, often cultivated, with green or purple leaves and small white or purplish flowers. Tulsi is known for its adaptogenic properties, helping the body manage stress and promoting overall well-being.

Aloevera

Common Names:True Aloe, Indian Aloe, or Barbados Aloe.
Scientific Name: Aloe barbadensis miller.
Aloe vera is a succulent plant known for its medicinal and cosmetic properties, particularly its soothing and healing gel. It's native to arid regions and has been used for centuries in various cultures for skin conditions, burns, and digestive issues. The gel contains vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products and remedies.
